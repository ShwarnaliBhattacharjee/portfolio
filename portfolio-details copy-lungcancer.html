• Category: ArcGIS StoryMap
  • Project date: Fall, 2022
  • Tools Used: Alteryx, Tableau, ArcGIS Pro
  • Project URL: https://storymaps.arcgis.com/LungCancer

As part of a Geospatial Data Visualization and Analytics project, I had the opportunity to create an ArcGIS StoryMap focused on assessing the spatial patterns of lung cancer incidence and mortality in North Central Texas. To achieve this, I utilized age-adjusted incidence and mortality data to determine race, gender, and age disparities in lung cancer incidence and mortality.

To help visualize this data, I developed a series of Tableau dashboards that highlighted lung cancer incidence and mortality rates by gender, race, and age. These dashboards provided an engaging and interactive way to explore the data and identify key patterns and trends. In addition to these dashboards, I also created a range of interactive maps that helped to illustrate the overall age-adjusted incidence rate, mortality rate, and incidence-mortality ratio in North Central Texas.

Overall, I believe that this project was an important contribution to the field of geospatial analysis and visualization. By highlighting the key disparities in lung cancer incidence and mortality across different demographics, we can gain important insights into the underlying factors driving these patterns and work to develop more effective interventions and public health strategies to address this critical issue.
